Legend in Action

For me, Arturo Gatti was the most spirited fighter in the entire history of boxing. His character was stronger than steel, and his willpower was admired in every one of his fights. He wasn't the most talented or super-technical boxer, but he won using his unbreakable character and perseverance. He became one of my favorite boxers after I saw the fight where he broke his hand but continued to fight, even though his opponent was superior to him in speed, strength, and technique. He didn't give up and continued to fight like a true warrior.

Facts from biography (BoxRec, ESPN, International Boxing Hall of Fame):

  • Born in Cassino, Italy, raised in Montreal, Canada.
  • Two-time world champion in two weight classes (lightweight and light welterweight).
  • Participated in the legendary trilogy against Micky Ward (2002-2003), which many experts call the greatest trilogy in boxing history. Fight of the Year by The Ring magazine all three times.
  • Finished his career with a record of 40-9 (31 KO). 21 of his fights were named "Fight of the Year" by various boxing publications.
  • Inducted into the International Boxing Hall of Fame in 2013.
  • Tragically died in 2009 in Brazil under unexplained circumstances.
  • Professional Debut: June 12, 1991
  • Final Bout: July 14, 2007
  • Championship Belts: IBF Junior Lightweight, WBC Super Lightweight
  • Total Fights: 49
  • Wins: 40 (KOs: 31)
  • Losses: 9
