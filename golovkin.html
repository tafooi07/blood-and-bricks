Legend in Action

From the provided biography, I see a phenomenal fighter who was missing in boxing. Statistics of 345 wins in amateurs out of 350 fights and an almost clean record in the pros speak for themselves. This is a boxer from God, whose discipline and power remind of the best representatives of sport.

Facts from biography (BoxRec, ESPN, official sources):

  • Born in Karaganda, Kazakhstan, to a mixed family (father Russian, mother Korean).
  • Amateur career: Olympic silver medalist (2004), world champion (2003), holder of numerous other international awards.
  • Professional career: Long-time middleweight world champion holding WBA (Super), WBC, IBF, and IBO titles. Set the record for highest knockout percentage among middleweight champions.
  • Defended his middleweight title 21 times, one of the best records in division history.
  • Fought two legendary battles against Saul "Canelo" Alvarez (draw and loss by decision), which became some of the highest-grossing in boxing history.
  • Known for his exceptionally disciplined lifestyle, family devotion, and signature fighting style with constant pressure and a powerful jab.
  • Professional Debut: May 6, 2006
  • Final Bout: September 28, 2024
  • Championship Belts: WBA (Super), WBC, IBF Middleweight
  • Total Fights: 45
  • Wins: 42 (KOs: 37)
  • Losses: 2
  • Draws: 1
