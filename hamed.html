Legend in Action

Naseem Hamed was a true superstar and showman. Thousands of people traveled to see not just boxing, but a real performance. His style, when he didn't just fight but played with his opponents, danced, and did backflips on the ring, was unique. He burned brighter than anyone, but his only loss in his professional career seemed to break his spirit. At the peak of his abilities, unable to overcome this, he burned out completely and left the sport.

Facts from biography (BoxRec, BBC, The Ring):

  • Born in Sheffield, England, to Yemeni immigrant parents.
  • World champion in bantamweight and featherweight divisions (WBO, IBF, WBC).
  • Known for his unorthodox style, phenomenal speed, and knockout power in his left hand.
  • Finished his career with an impressive record of 36-1 (31 KO).
  • His only loss came in 2001 against Marco Antonio Barrera. After that fight, Hamed had only two more fights over three years and retired in 2002 at age 28.
  • His famous ring entrances, including jumping over the ropes, and eccentric behavior made him one of the most colorful and recognizable figures in 1990s boxing.
  • Professional Debut: February 19, 1992
  • Final Bout: May 18, 2002
  • Championship Belts: WBO Featherweight, IBF Featherweight, WBC Featherweight
  • Total Fights: 37
  • Wins: 36 (KOs: 31)
  • Losses: 1
